What the correction guarantees

The yellow hover or selection overlay is one silhouette. Its stroke follows the outside perimeter only; it does not outline the body, cap, circle, or side-line components where they overlap inside the highlighted area.

Scope: this is a drawing-only correction. Endpoint decorations remain part of the filled highlight and exact hit area. Real strand geometry is not removed.

The source is the current step (step 18) of masknotworkingexample.json. It contains six layers. Each sample below is a plain snapshot, so it can be loaded directly without importing the original history file.

Exact before and after

The old overlay passed a composite path and a pen directly to Qt. Because that path contains overlapping subpaths, Qt stroked every boundary. The corrected renderer paints the fill separately and derives an outside-only ring.

Technical design

  1. Keep body and endpoint footprints as independently usable components for reliable hit-testing.
  2. Append them to a winding-fill path; do not rely on Qt’s fragile Boolean union at touching angled edges.
  3. Stroke the composite footprint wider than the desired border.
  4. Subtract the filled footprint from that stroke.
  5. Paint the translucent selection fill, then paint the remaining exterior ring.
